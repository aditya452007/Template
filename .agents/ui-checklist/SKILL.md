# UI Checklist Skill

A comprehensive set of design checklists for building complete, polished user interfaces. Organized into **Website Pages**, **Components**, **Flows**, and **Topics**. Use these to audit existing designs, plan new features, or ensure nothing is missed during implementation.

---

## How to Use This Skill

1. **Identify what you're building** — a page (Pricing, Login), a component (Button, Modal), or a flow (Adding to Cart, Resetting Password)
2. **Open the relevant section** below and go through every item
3. **Mark each item as done** or note what's missing in your current design
4. **For cross-cutting concerns**, also check the **Topics** section (Dark Mode, UX Copy, Responsiveness)

# WEBSITE PAGES

## Billing
- [ ] **Payment method/s used** — Display payment method details with ability to update or remove. Subscription services should prevent all methods being removed.
- [ ] **History** — List of past charges with ability to download invoices (PDF format).
- [ ] **Plan details** — Current plan info includes price, features, and next billing date. Clear action to upgrade or change plan.
- [ ] **Promo or discount code** — Field to apply a code to activate a discount or deal.
- [ ] **Additional details** — Info that influences billing decisions (seats, roles, usage, metrics).
- [ ] **Next payment** — Upcoming payment details (amount, date, method).
- [ ] **Cancel subscription** — Clear action to cancel and stop payments.

## Team
- [ ] **Personality and branding** — Theme/tone that conveys how serious or fun your team is.
- [ ] **Team members** — Representation of your team, scaled by team size.
- [ ] **Visuals** — Photography or illustrations of team, offices, or culture.
- [ ] **Group by area or team** — Divide members by department for easier scanning.
- [ ] **Link to careers** — Connect team page to job openings.

## Cart
- [ ] **Item name and image** — Exactly as on the item page.
- [ ] **Change or remove item** — Edit items inside the cart.
- [ ] **Item variations** — Size, colour, style, type with alternative shipping methods and costs.
- [ ] **Apply promo, discount or coupon** — Obvious text field above checkout button, with success/decline status.
- [ ] **Accepted payment methods** — Secure payment provider logos to instill trust.
- [ ] **Link to support** — For issues or questions about items.
- [ ] **Link to checkout** — Standout action to proceed to purchase.
- [ ] **Suggested items to add** — Upsell relevant items.

## Search
- [ ] **Search box** — At top of page, styled consistently. Include submit button and helpful placeholder text.
- [ ] **Visualisation of results** — Show title, description, thumbnail. Display result count. Include pagination for 50+ results. Handle zero results with guidance.
- [ ] **Search progress** — Loading indicator for non-instant searches.
- [ ] **Suggested search items** — Trending, popular, recent searches, autocorrection.
- [ ] **Categories and/or filters** — Constraints to narrow down search area.
- [ ] **Ordering of search results** — Sort by price, relevance, newest, etc.
- [ ] **Highlighting keywords** — Highlight search terms in results.

## Careers
- [ ] **Company story** — How the business started, the why and motivations.
- [ ] **Up to date information** — Especially quantitative values (headcount, offices, time operating).
- [ ] **Employee benefits** — Tangible value props for potential employees.
- [ ] **Teams and divisions** — Team breakdown scaled by company size.
- [ ] **Job openings** — All current roles, broken by department.
- [ ] **Working locations** — Office locations and remote work options.
- [ ] **Employee testimonials** — Stories from current employees.

## Blog Post
- [ ] **Title** — Educational, eye-catching copy.
- [ ] **Sub heading** — Elaborates on the title.
- [ ] **Thumbnail** — Branded visual aid.
- [ ] **Author** — Writer attribution, linked to their profile.
- [ ] **Content** — Blog post body, responsive on all devices.
- [ ] **Social sharing tools** — Fixed-position share buttons.
- [ ] **Lead conversion content** — Product info tied to the post.
- [ ] **Related articles** — Suggested relevant posts.
- [ ] **Opt-in for subscription** — Signup form for new post notifications.
- [ ] **Date posted** — Publication date.

## Contact Us
- [ ] **Personality and branding** — Visual character and tone of voice aligned with brand.
- [ ] **Clear methods to contact** — Phone, email, or form.
- [ ] **Social media accounts** — Alternative contact via social platforms.
- [ ] **Segmenting contact methods** — Different contacts for support vs sales.
- [ ] **Easy location to get to** — Available in header or footer.

## Pricing
- [ ] **Pricing options** — Subscription plans or one-off purchase.
- [ ] **Pricing features** — What the user gets with each option.
- [ ] **A free pathway to sign up** — Trial period with clear length and billing terms.
- [ ] **Refund or return policy** — Clear terms.
- [ ] **Highlighted price as a recommendation** — Most popular or best-value option highlighted.
- [ ] **Security logo for payment processing** — Credible vendor logo.
- [ ] **Frequency of payments (monthly vs yearly)** — Annual discount option.

## FAQ
- [ ] **Purposeful information** — Questions and answers that provide real value.
- [ ] **Contact options** — Method for users whose question isn't answered.
- [ ] **Table of contents** — For large question sets, navigate by topic.
- [ ] **Search functionality** — Keyword search for questions.
- [ ] **Navigate by topic** — Segment questions into general topics.

## 404
- [ ] **Logo** — Complete logo or symbol mark.
- [ ] **Title** — Clear indication user is on the 404 page.
- [ ] **Description** — Explain why the user landed here.
- [ ] **Links to other pages** — Homepage link, contact link, search bar.
- [ ] **Illustrations, patterns, visual flair** — Show brand personality.

## Login Page
- [ ] **Logo** — Complete logo or symbol mark.
- [ ] **Title** — Tell the user this is the login page.
- [ ] **Account identification** — Username, email, or phone number. Consider multiple identifiers.
- [ ] **Password** — Secure input with visibility toggle.
- [ ] **Link to reset password** — Positioned near the password field.
- [ ] **Link to sign up** — For users without an account.
- [ ] **Login via third party** — Google, Apple, Facebook, Twitter, etc.
- [ ] **Testimonial** — Brand reinforcement.
- [ ] **Blog post** — Relevant content showcase.
- [ ] **New feature** — Build excitement before login.

## Blog
- [ ] **Hero** — Clear indicator it's a blog.
- [ ] **Navigation** — Logo, links, CTA, search function. Sub-topic navigation.
- [ ] **Layout** — Consider above-the-fold on mobile, tablet, desktop. Newest-first ordering. Grid layout for density.
- [ ] **Blog post preview** — Clear heading hierarchy, branded thumbnail, concise description.
- [ ] **Description** — Summary of blog in the hero.
- [ ] **Tags and/or categories** — Filterable topics.
- [ ] **Search function** — For large blog archives.
- [ ] **Opt-in for subscription** — Notify users of new posts.

## Sign Up
- [ ] **Logo** — Complete logo or symbol mark.
- [ ] **Title** — Tell the user this is where they create an account.
- [ ] **Description** — Basic features and expectations of account creation.
- [ ] **Account identification** — Email is most common, also enables communication.
- [ ] **Setting a password** — Include password strength indicator, requirements, and visibility toggle.
- [ ] **Link to login** — For users who already have an account.
- [ ] **Sign up via third party** — Facebook, Google, etc.
- [ ] **Current active customer count** — Social proof with FOMO.
- [ ] **Testimonial or social proof** — Brand reinforcement.
- [ ] **Blog post** — Show relevant content to convert unsure leads.

# COMPONENTS

## Drawer
- [ ] **Placement** — Which edge the drawer slides from (left for navigation, right for details/settings).
- [ ] **Dimensions** — Full height, width sufficient for content without taking over the entire page.
- [ ] **Overlay** — Semi-transparent background to bring focus to the drawer.
- [ ] **Header** — Title describing contents, optional close action and description.
- [ ] **Content area** — Scrollable main section.
- [ ] **Open and close trigger** — Button to open; overlay click, close button, or Esc key to close.
- [ ] **Footer** — Fixed bottom area for primary/secondary actions (Save & Cancel).

## Accordion
- [ ] **Header** — Clickable trigger with title that clearly conveys content inside.
- [ ] **Expand/collapse icon** — Plus/minus or caret/chevron that rotates.
- [ ] **Content area** — Shows/hides on toggle; can contain text, image, video, etc.
- [ ] **States** — Default (collapsed), expanded, hover, focused, disabled.
- [ ] **Expanding logic** — Multiple sections open at once vs one at a time.

## Skeleton
- [ ] **Match content structure** — Mirror size and layout of actual content.
- [ ] **Animation** — Subtle shimmer, pulse, or wave motion.
- [ ] **Style** — Flat shapes, rounded or sharp corners.
- [ ] **Color scheme** — Neutral gray or soft colour, just enough contrast with background.
- [ ] **Transition to real content** — Smooth fade out before content loads.

## Footer
- [ ] **Size and responsiveness** — Full width or contained.
- [ ] **Style** — Colours, font sizes, visual brand elements. Contrast with body content.
- [ ] **Content links** — Visuals, states, and destinations of all links.
- [ ] **Legal and compliance links** — Privacy policy, terms, legal content.
- [ ] **Logo or name of business** — Brand consistency, homepage link. Optional tagline.
- [ ] **Hierarchy and layout** — Balanced between logo, links, and actions. Group links into meaningful categories.
- [ ] **Social media** — Logos/links to active social platforms.
- [ ] **Mobile app download actions** — Apple/Google store links.
- [ ] **Trust/security badges** — Certifications and security measures.

## Carousel
- [ ] **Ways of interaction** — Auto-play, manual navigation, or both.
- [ ] **Visual progress indicator** — Dots, numbers, or progress bar. Current slide should look distinct.
- [ ] **Dimensions and alignment** — Size and responsiveness of items. Content fills space without stretching.
- [ ] **Animation behaviour** — Direction, speed, and feel of transitions.
- [ ] **Focused item state** — Non-focused items differ in size, transparency, or colour.

## Banner
- [ ] **Style** — Fill, text colour, border radius. Must stand out from default UI.
- [ ] **Content** — Title is essential; optional description.
- [ ] **Types** — Information, success, warning, error, neutral.
- [ ] **Call to action button** — Optional, only if user action is needed.
- [ ] **Placement** — Page position based on context and importance.
- [ ] **Dismissable** — Positive/neutral banners can be dismissed; warning/error should not.

## Slider
- [ ] **Style** — Visual design of range and handlers.
- [ ] **Intervals** — Continuous (any value) or stepped (fixed values).
- [ ] **Handles** — Elements that users drag. Should clearly break out from the container.
- [ ] **Labelling** — Text conveying the spectrum and interval values.
- [ ] **Interaction states** — Handle and slider changes during value adjustment.

## Button
- [ ] **Base style** — Fill, outline, or underline.
- [ ] **Shape** — Padding, border, border radius, shadow.
- [ ] **Variants** — Visual types (primary, secondary, etc.).
- [ ] **Copy** — Instructional text explaining what will happen on click.
- [ ] **States** — Hover, focused, disabled.

## Badge
- [ ] **Detail** — Numeric value or basic shape (circle for updates).
- [ ] **Color** — Error, success, warning states. Consistent outlines, shadows, corners.
- [ ] **Offset position** — Sit outside the relevant element for attention.

## Avatar
- [ ] **Visualiser** — Show what the avatar looks like before posting.
- [ ] **Link to upload or select avatar** — Clickable with editing indicator (pencil icon).
- [ ] **Placeholder image** — Default icon or user initials.
- [ ] **Acceptable file types** — JPEG, PNG, SVG.
- [ ] **Status change updating avatar** — Banner or toast confirmation.
- [ ] **Alternatives** — Illustrative option if user doesn't want to upload.
- [ ] **Crop and resize** — Allow cropping and resizing before saving.

## Input Field
- [ ] **Input field** — Interactive text field, readable on all devices.
- [ ] **Label** — States what information to provide. Avoid all caps.
- [ ] **Placeholder text** — Example of what to enter. Colour must be lighter than default text.
- [ ] **Data format** — Restrict to relevant values (numeric for phone). Hint at format.
- [ ] **Illustration or icon** — Visual cue to break up long forms.
- [ ] **Hint** — Elaborate on the label if users may struggle.

## Icon
- [ ] **Responsiveness** — Detail level appropriate for all display sizes.
- [ ] **Visual style consistency** — Same stroke weight, corner radius, optical sizing.
- [ ] **Color** — Black/white, flat colors, or gradients. Brand-consistent.
- [ ] **Naming** — Name by what it literally is, not by action (e.g. "pencil" not "edit").

## Table
- [ ] **Table header** — Column values providing structure. Fix header on scroll.
- [ ] **Row style** — Borders and contrasting background colours for differentiation.
- [ ] **Spacing** — Consistent padding for each row and header.
- [ ] **Search** — Find specific keywords or rows across all data points.
- [ ] **Actions** — View, edit, delete per row.
- [ ] **Filter and sort** — Customize visible data and ordering.
- [ ] **Responsiveness** — Collapse to accordion or horizontal scroll on small devices.
- [ ] **Pagination** — Breakpoints for digesting large data sets.

## Card
- [ ] **Style** — Background, border, shadow.
- [ ] **Consistency** — One base style for all cards.
- [ ] **Spacing** — Framework for padding levels (divisible by 4 or 5).
- [ ] **Responsiveness** — Top-down approach on mobile to avoid overfilled rows.
- [ ] **Content hierarchy** — Primary actions clear. Links at end of card content.

## Searchbar
- [ ] **Input field** — Clear container for typing.
- [ ] **Label or placeholder text** — Identify purpose. Suggest search examples.
- [ ] **Quick links, autocomplete and suggestions** — Offer based on typed input or past searches.
- [ ] **Submit search button** — Visible action with loading indicator for connection issues.
- [ ] **Previous searches** — Speed up repeat queries.
- [ ] **Search scope** — Whole platform or specific area.

## Tooltip
- [ ] **Information** — Clear, concise, entirely relevant. Elaborates on visible content.
- [ ] **Contrast** — Inverse colour from underlying content (light on dark or dark on light).
- [ ] **Visibility** — Activates on hover/select. Only one visible at a time. Does not cover relevant content.
- [ ] **Dismiss action** — For intrusive or extensive tooltips.

## Modal
- [ ] **Title** — Clear, simple text explaining the action.
- [ ] **Actionable item** — Button/link to continue or close. Clear button copy or descriptive label.
- [ ] **Close action** — Way to exit the modal.
- [ ] **Responsiveness** — Full-screen on mobile.
- [ ] **Background change behind modal** — Darken, blur, or lighten to bring focus.
- [ ] **Description** — Extra information for decision-making.

## Loading
- [ ] **Visual indicator** — Clear representation that content is loading.
- [ ] **Text** — Explain the loading state. Be specific ("syncing your emails", not just "loading").
- [ ] **Time** — Determine delay threshold for showing loading component.
- [ ] **Accessibility** — Visible on any background. Container with appropriate contrast.
- [ ] **Visuals** — Entertain user with illustration during load.

## Toggle
- [ ] **Context** — Explains what the toggle does. Always turns a function on/off (not between two opposing versions).
- [ ] **Transition** — Clear visual change between states.
- [ ] **State** — Active vs inactive contrast. Brand colour for active, muted for inactive.

## Checkbox
- [ ] **Label** — Text indicating what is enabled if selected.
- [ ] **Default selection** — Active by default only if it sufficiently conveniences the user.
- [ ] **Style** — Unique from other inputs. Consider container, grouping, colour.
- [ ] **States** — Default, hover, focused, active, disabled.

## Radio
- [ ] **Label** — Text associated with the radio input.
- [ ] **Grouping** — Must have more than one option. Vertical alignment preferred for longer labels.
- [ ] **Default selection** — Start with a likely option selected based on research.
- [ ] **Style** — Unique from other inputs.
- [ ] **Clickability** — Clickable text label and container.
- [ ] **States** — Default, active, disabled.

## Navigation
- [ ] **Logo** — Reinforce brand on every page.
- [ ] **Structure** — Most important links at high level. Two levels max hierarchy. Distinct visual design per level.
- [ ] **Consistent location** — Always at the same position on every page.
- [ ] **Visual contrast to rest of page** — Clearly distinguishable. Consider shadow/background change on scroll.
- [ ] **Call to action/s** — Primary actions structured by importance.
- [ ] **Mobile responsiveness** — Dedicated mobile menu design.
- [ ] **Icons next to link** — Visual cue to break up text.
- [ ] **Breadcrumbs** — Show current level and hierarchy above.

## Toast
- [ ] **Copy** — Clear and concise, focusing on status or action.
- [ ] **Placement** — Corners of the viewport, not as the focus.
- [ ] **Usage** — Triggered after an action or event.
- [ ] **Variants** — Colour-coded (green for success, red for error). Don't rely solely on colour; use icons or copy.
- [ ] **Length of appearance** — Long enough to read, short enough to not obstruct. Read aloud to gauge.
- [ ] **Dismissable** — User can close; should fade away shortly if not manually dismissed.

## Tabs
- [ ] **Labels** — Logical enough to predict tab content. Keep concise, ideally 1 word.
- [ ] **Content area** — Below horizontal tabs or to the right of vertical tabs.
- [ ] **Style** — Active vs inactive visual differentiation.
- [ ] **Item order** — Ordered by popularity or familiarity.
- [ ] **States** — Default, active, hover.

# FLOWS

## Adding to Cart
- [ ] **Outline variant selection for product** — Size, colour, amount must be clearly selectable.
- [ ] **Primary action is add to cart** — Most prominent button on the page.
- [ ] **Feedback once added** — Immediate confirmation the item was added.
- [ ] **Link to cart view** — In primary navigation. Show item count and optionally total price.

## Uploading Media
- [ ] **Empty state** — Visual placeholder with upload icon and text. Offer click-to-upload alternative.
- [ ] **Drag and drop interaction** — Clear visual state change when file is detected over the area.
- [ ] **Progress indicator** — Real-time progress. Show file names and overall progress for multiple files.
- [ ] **File restrictions & constraints** — Size and format limits shown upfront. Error message for invalid files.
- [ ] **Outcome status** — Green checkmark for success, red warning for failure with explanation.
- [ ] **Upload actions** — Retry, cancel, delete, rename. Default vs hover visibility.
- [ ] **Showing multiple uploaded files** — Clean list or grid with thumbnails. Relevant file info shown.

## Verifying Account
- [ ] **Establish a trigger point** — Clear indication when and why verification is required.
- [ ] **Method selection (optional)** — Multiple verification methods (email, SMS, authenticator).
- [ ] **Confirm delivery and contact information used** — Display destination (email/phone) for user confirmation.
- [ ] **Ability to input verification code** — Intuitive input (one field per digit or standard field).
- [ ] **Incorrect value (and resend option)** — Specific error messages for expired/invalid/max-attempts. Clear next steps.
- [ ] **Verification success state** — Clear confirmation and transition to next step.

## Canceling Subscription
- [ ] **Show a link in account details** — In account page or billing settings. Clearly visible.
- [ ] **Confirm intent to cancel** — Ask for confirmation. Optional last-chance value reminder (not pressuring).
- [ ] **Request a reason for canceling (optional)** — Useful feedback for identifying churn factors.
- [ ] **Confirm subscription has been canceled** — Tell user when their subscription is active until.

## Filtering Items
- [ ] **Show action near item collection** — Above or beside the collection. Recognizable icon and/or label.
- [ ] **Show available filter options** — Same page (immediate feedback) or separate page (large filter sets). Priority order.
- [ ] **Consider different filter types** — Sliders, checkboxes, dropdowns. Pick the easiest for each property.
- [ ] **Show active filters clearly when applied** — Display applied filters on collection page.
- [ ] **Provide easy filter removal** — Clear individual filters or all filters at once.
- [ ] **Show result count** — Help users gauge if result set is too narrow or broad.
- [ ] **Empty state** — Explain no results and suggest adjusting/clearing filters.

## Saving Changes
- [ ] **Show action that enables change** — Button triggers editable state if not auto-editable.
- [ ] **Disable save action until changes are made** — Disabled initially, activates on change. Fixed position recommended.
- [ ] **State changes to active once a change is made** — Brings attention to the save action.
- [ ] **Action changes to loading state when pressed** — Loading spinner in the action.
- [ ] **Notify changes have been saved** — Clear confirmation that data is locked in.

## Entering Promo Code
- [ ] **Show promo code input field** — In cart or checkout, visible near the continue button.
- [ ] **Promo code applied successfully** — Success state colours (green). Hide input if only one code allowed.
- [ ] **Reflect promo code impact** — Show discount as total amount or per-item. Repeat near checkout total.
- [ ] **Promo code error** — Not applicable or incorrect code handling.
- [ ] **Remove promo code** — Allow user to revert to original price or use a different code.

## Showing Input Error
- [ ] **Keep the input in default state** — Check for errors only after information is entered.
- [ ] **Allow user to enter information** — No interruptions while typing, don't assess mid-input.
- [ ] **Signal error after loss of focus** — Explain why the error happened and what's required. Combine text with error icon.
- [ ] **Return to default state upon reattempt** — Error message disappears on focus. Process repeats if error persists.

## Resetting Password
- [ ] **Place reset link close to password field** — Styled as a clickable link.
- [ ] **Ask for account details to verify** — Email address (pre-fill from login page if available).
- [ ] **Show information has been sent** — Explain how to continue (email with link, or code to phone).
- [ ] **The message sent explains next steps** — Link to reset page or verification code entry.
- [ ] **Reset the password!** — Clear text field for new password. Guidelines for strength requirements.
- [ ] **Password successfully reset** — Indicate success and direct user to log in.

## Deleting Account
- [ ] **Show a link to delete account** — In profile/settings and support area. Make it accessible.
- [ ] **Politely ask for feedback** — Request reason for leaving to improve experience. Not pressured.
- [ ] **Explain what it means before confirming** — Data deletion, restoration possibility, deletion timeline.
- [ ] **Confirm account has been deleted** — Acknowledge departure gracefully. No passive aggressiveness.

## Contacting Support
- [ ] **Show a link to contact support** — Footer, settings, error pages. Clear copy and visuals.
- [ ] **Show methods of contact** — List options (chat, call, FAQ) with benefits. FAQ first for self-service.
- [ ] **Outline how to communicate and what is expected** — Response time and required info.

## Making a Payment
- [ ] **Offer payment methods** — Credit/debit card with other third-party options.
- [ ] **Request card details** — Relevant text fields sized appropriately. Support saved payment methods.
- [ ] **Submit payment with card details entered** — Validate before submission. Handle error states (mistyped numbers, expired cards).
- [ ] **Show payment processing** — Loading page/indicator during processing.
- [ ] **Confirmation payment processed successfully** — Clear success indicator.
- [ ] **Outline next steps** — What happens next (order in motion, link to product).

## Submitting a Form
- [ ] **Show button to submit** — Below form fields. Copy matches form purpose ("Subscribe", "Create Account").
- [ ] **Show loading state after submission** — Loading indicator. Consider hover state before press.
- [ ] **Show success message when it submits** — Clear success confirmation.
- [ ] **If it doesn't, show an error message** — Clear error communication.
- [ ] **Error may occur because of wrong information** — Detail which field failed and why.

# TOPICS

## Dark Mode
- [ ] **Aligned colours switching with light mode (variables)** — System of colours to invert for easy transition.
- [ ] **Brand integrity** — Maintain brand through primary, non-neutral colours.
- [ ] **Elevation** — Establish hierarchy without shadows; use borders and lightness instead.
- [ ] **Switch to light mode** — Toggle at global level or in profile/settings.

## UX Copy
- [ ] **Clear and concise** — Say exactly what will happen in as few words as possible.
- [ ] **Purposeful** — Every word has intent. User understands action before interaction.
- [ ] **Tone** — Consistent with brand. Some brands benefit from humour, others don't.
- [ ] **Conventions** — Maintain one term per action (don't swap "Next" and "Continue").
- [ ] **Avoid dark patterns** — No sarcasm, antagonism, or guilt tripping to influence decisions.

## Responsiveness
- [ ] **Fluid layout** — Content accessible on any device. Grids and columns for proportional content.
- [ ] **Breakpoints** — Identify key points: mobile, tablet, desktop, ultra wide.
- [ ] **Typography** — Scale text sizes across breakpoints (H1 differs on desktop vs mobile).
- [ ] **Selection area for actions** — Larger touch targets on mobile, finer accuracy on desktop.
- [ ] **Hierarchy** — Adapt content and visual structure per screen size.
